CROATIA



Dubrovnik



On the left is the island of Lokrum. On the right is Dubrovnik's Old City. Most of the buildings have bright-orange roofs since the original roofs were destroyed during the 1990s armed conflict.




A view of the Old City from the walls of Dubrovnik.




Another view of Old City but from the south side this time. I took this picture while stopped at an intersection in a moped. If you visit Dubrovnik, make sure to rent a scooter. It will help you get to some amazing views!




High above St. Jakov's beach. There are 200-some-odd stairs leading down to St. Jakov's beach, but it is worth everyone of them. The views are stunning from this pebble beach.




And look at how clear the water is!




Old City Dubrovnik in late afternoon. There are a lot of tourists traps in this area, because of the high volume of visitors, but it is an amazing place to walk around and take in views.




This is another shot from the Hotel Stari Grad Restaurant in Old City. It is one of the pricier restaurants around but the views are unmatched. Our food was excellent as well.




A shot facing south of the Old City. This was also taken on my scooter ride around Dubrovnik.




Walking down one of the long set of steps that is so characteristic of Dubrovnik.




This was the view out of the front door of our flat that we rented through airbnb. We had updated furniture, air conditioning and even a moped that we got to borrow for free. All for around $85 a night!




Dubrovnik's Nearby Islands



This a picture of one of the iconic Dubrovnik sunsets while leaving Lopud, an island near Dubrovnik. There are several ferries between Dubrovnik, Kolocep and Lopud. If you like sandy beaches, Lopud is likely a place you might want to visit.




Kolocep is another one of the beautiful islands near Dubrovnik, and it's on the same ferry route. Crystal clear water, beaches, and a relaxed atmosphere are some of the reasons to visit this little island.
