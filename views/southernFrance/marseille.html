MARSEILLE, FRANCE

Marseille is France's second city. Still with a population of under a million, Marseille is about twice as big as any other city in France, aside from Paris. It has always been an important port and harbor, and today its Vieux Port (the Old Port) is still a very charming area, and a hot spot for activity. Marseille's long history of wealth is evident throughout the city. One of the more underrated aspects of Marseille, in my opinion, is the proximity to calanques. A calanque is a narrow, steep-walled inlet that was developed in limestone or dolimite, and it is where the locals like to go to escape the city, sunbathe, and take in amazing views.

VIEUX PORT

Vieux Port, which means Old Port, is probably the most popular attraction in Marseille, and with good reason. It's such a great area for strolling, enjoying a happy hour on the terrace, and exploring around.

CALANQUE DE NIOLON

Calanque De Niolon is one of the closest calanques to Marseille. There are perhaps better ones, but for a 35 min train ride, it was more than adequate.

There were maybe 50ish people spread out around the entire calanque. We brought a few beers, some Caprice Des Dieux(which you HAVE to try if you go to a French supermarket), and some Bonne Maman jam. There aren't a ton of shops nearby so make sure to prepare well.
