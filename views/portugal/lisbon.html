LISBON REGION, PORTUGAL

When I first got to Lisbon, I wasn't really impressed. It comes off a bit rougher than a lot of European cities. It quickly won me over, though. Lisbon had great food, was cheap, and very close to a lot of cool towns. But, the thing that I remember most fondly was how we met so many generous, friendly people. From Lisbon, I traveled to Cascais for a day(location of Praia do Guincho, or "James Bond Beach", if you will. --I WILL) and the amazing Sintra for a day.

LISBON

Lisbon, like most of Portugal, is very hilly and produces fantastic views of the city.

Look a lot like the Golden Gate Bridge? The 25 de Abril bridge, pictured, was actually built by The American Bridge Company, which oddly enough built the Bay bridge, but not the Golden Gate.

Barrio Alto in Lisbon.

Lisbon had great food. One thing you have to try is hot pasteis de nata.

CASCAIS

Praia do Guincho, in Cascais. This was the beach in the opening scene of the 1969 James Bond film, On Her Majesty's Secret Service

While walking along the beach, we spotted this little hideaway in between two cliffs. I could have stayed here forever.

SINTRA

Sintra is one of my favorite places of all-time. There is so much to see in such a small, gorgeous town. Pictured is Pena Palace.

The palace is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of Portugal.

Classic Terri being daring on the walls of Pena Palace, Sintra.

Pena National Park, Sintra. The whole thing looks prehistoric to me. The vegetation is unreal.

Just down the road from Pena Palace is Quinta da Regaleira. There are numerous waterfalls, statues, and underground tunnels linking the important areas.
