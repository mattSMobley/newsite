PORTO, PORTUGAL

Terri and I met a couple of friends from London in Porto for 4 days. We did some port wine tours, took cable cars and funiculars, walked to the coast and had fresh fish, and spent a lot of time down by the Douro river. Porto is a very underrated European destination. If you go here, you HAVE to try the francesinha, it is so delicious and horrible for you.

AROUND PORTO

Porto has an extremely sloped terrain. It makes short walks seem a bit longer sometimes. Although, the plus side is amazing views when you reach the top of a hill.

The Portuguese term for these fantastic viewpoints are "miradouros."

Livraria Lello. The inspiration for some Harry Potter building, I've heard.

We are in Southern Europe, so here is a beautiful church.

DOURO RIVER

The Douro River and Gaia, the side of the river from which this picture was taken.

The Douro is an excellent place to grab a snack and do some people watching.

We stopped at one of the restaurants along the Douro and had a $5 meal including a soup, sandwich, and glass of wine.

DOM LUIS BRIDGE on the DOURO

I took this picture sitting outside of bar with a few friends. They were offering $6 pitchers of sangria. Not bad.

The famous Dom Luis Bridge. You can take the upper road or the lower.

FOZ DO DOURO

The Foz do Douro. Lovely area with great, fresh seafood.

FOOD & DRINK

Port Wine tasting. Porto is where port wine comes from, so ya kinda HAVE to do a tasting.

THE francesinha. Layer of bread, layer of ham, layer of salami, layer of cheese, layer of thin steak, layer of bread, layer of cheese, layer fried egg, layer of bacon, and smothered in beer-tomato sauce!
