MALAGA, SPAIN

I've been to Malaga twice, but only for one full day each time. However, when you only have one day in a place, you seem to do as much as you can. Malaga was no different. Still, there's so much more I would like to see. Each time in Malaga, I went in without an itinerary and stumbled across excellent cafes, cool beachfront restaurants, Roman coloseums, Moorish gardens, amazing museums, and more.

PLAZA DE TOROS

On the way up to Castillo de Gibralfaro, there are excellent panoramic views of Malagueta, the beach/port area of Malaga.

You can view some of the pretty, unique parks of Malagueta from here.

When you get to the top of Castillo de Gibralfaro, you can see ALL of Malaga.

You can see for miles.

ALCAZABA AREA

After checking out Castillo de Gibralfaro, you should take the trail back down to Alcazaba. The Alcazaba is a collection of fountains and gardens, with views of some of the nicest parts of Malaga, like this.

DOWNTOWN PEDESTRIAN MALL

The downtown pedestrian mall area, to me, is one of the nicest areas in Spain. There are fancy shops, cafes, and museums(Like the Picasso Museum).

It is a charming area with carriage rides and flower displays.

MALAGUETA

Malagueta, the port/beach area of Malaga, is another charming area. There are upscale boutiques and colorful restaurants with views of the port and it's many sailboats.

From the port, there is a little covered trail leading to the beach, where you can find seafood tapas and sangria galore.
