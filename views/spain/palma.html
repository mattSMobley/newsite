PALMA DE MALLORCA, SPAIN

Palma is a beautiful port city that has been used as a vacation spot for kings and queens throughout Spanish history. The port, the beach, and the gorgeous Serra de Tramuntana mountain range make Palma a city with a lot of different types of entertainment.

LA SEU

La Seu, which means cathedral, in Palma is one of the premiere attractions. It is a magnificent work of architecture right near the port and the beach in Palma.

At night, they light it up.

And nearby, there is always some sort of festival. I was quite lucky and the Palma Beer Festival was going on when I was there in July.

PARC DE LA MAR

I love walking around the many parks in Spain. There are always beautiful, native flowers and well groomed trails.

PALACIO REAL DE LA ALMUDAINA

Right next to the cathedral and the park is an area called Palacio Real de La Almudaina. It has some neat little park areas and is a good spot to walk around.

PORT DE PALMA

The port of Palma is very impressive. It is packed to the brim with sailboats of all kinds.

And it is quite charming at night. Here is a view with Castell de Bellver in the distance.

PORT DE SOLLER

About an hour away from Palma is a little port town called Port De Soller. It is very resort-y and surprisingly nice. Being on the edge of the Serra de Tramuntana mountains, there are some excellent trails around for viewing the port from a higher vantage point, or you could just sunbathe on the beach.

Along the port, there is a famous cable car that is popular with tourists.
