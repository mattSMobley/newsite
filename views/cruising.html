CRUISING

I've been on a handful of Caribbean cruises and two transatlantic cruises. I've always loved cruises, I think you get a lot for your money. But transatlantic cruises are another story. Both of the transatlantic cruises I've been on were under $500 per ticket. Not only is that less than the price of a one-way flight to Europe, but you also get to go to several amazing ports, get gourmet food, maid service, and a lot of time to relax/tan/work-out/read/etc.

PHILIPSBURG, SINT MAARTEN

The beach at Philipsburg is the prettiest I've ever been to.

FUNCHAL, MADEIRA

I would definitely go back to Funchal for a longer visit. It was a beautiful island city with a ton to offer.

TENERIFE, CANARY ISLANDS

I wouldn't rate Tenerife as highly as Funchal, but it still is very charming. There are parks and flowers and beautiful views everywhere. Also, there is a national park where you can ride ATVs and such.

GIBRALTAR, U.K.

We stopped in Gibraltar for a day on a transatlantic cruise.

MALAGA, SPAIN

I've been to Malaga twice on transatlantic cruises. It has a reputation in Europe of being an overcrowded British retirement hotspot, but I absolutely loved it. It's pedestrian mall is extremely nice and the beach isn't too crowded.
